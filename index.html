Biography of Joeshua Villarta

Childhood Games to College Goals

Childhood Years

I was born and raised in a family filled with love and life lessons, and I am originally from Tandang Sora, Quezon City.1 In my daily life as a child, I was always full of joy and happiness because during those times, I had no responsibilities to think about or carry.2 Back then, all I ever thought about was what games I would play throughout the day.3 I would often find ways to escape the house in the afternoon because I was forced to take naps, as my parents believed that sleeping in the afternoon would help me grow faster.4 But because I was stubborn and eager to play with my friends, I disobeyed my parents’ instructions to sleep, and I was often punished for it.5 I was also the kind of child who was always out in the streets because that was where I spent time with other kids who brought me happiness.6 This was also the time when I learned how to interact and get along with others.7 Being a kid who was always out in the streets encouraged me to try the things a child should experience, and one of those was playing traditional Filipino games.8 In games like patintero, luksong baka, and tumbang preso, I was able to show my agility and my resourceful way of playing.9 There were also many times when I got involved in fights whenever the games got too heated.10 I often went home with bruises and bumps because of how physical and tense the games could get.11 Because of this, my parents would often scold me and remind me that they didn’t raise me to be a troublemaker, so I should never get into fights.12 My family taught me how to be cheerful and how to bring happiness to others.13 They taught me that I should always stay positive in life because being sad will never bring me anything good.14 They also taught me to have fear and respect for God.15 They always reminded me not to take advantage of others and to treat the people around me the right way, because we are all equal in life.<.16 And when it comes to school, I can say that I became quite careless with my studies despite the constant guidance and support of my parents.17 Those were the times when I still didn’t see the importance of focusing on my education because my attention was still on playing, as those things made me happier.18 At a young age, I learned different ways to bring joy and make my family happy.19 And this was the time when I started realizing that I can do more things.20


Teenage Years

My teenage years were full of challenges, changes, and self-reflection.1 It was during this time that I had deep realizations about myself and my plans for the future.2 I also felt fear of the possible challenges I would face because I didn’t know if I could handle them or not.3 During these times, I had no certainty about the path I would take, as I was too focused on playing online games, which were one of the things that brought me happiness.4 I also met many new friends who became my companions in life; they are an important part of my journey because they shared both the good and bad experiences with me, which became lessons for all of us.5 As my teenage years went on, I realized that I needed to become more mature in the things I do.6 This was because my responsibilities in life and school were slowly increasing.7 I learned to be more disciplined and to value the things I hold important in life—my time at school, with my family, my friends, and for myself.8 I also became more serious about my studies because I learned the importance of education.9 When I was in elementary school, I prioritized playing over studying.10 But in high school, I focused more on my studies because I saw my potential to become an academic achiever, since I didn’t receive any academic awards when I was younger.11 I also realized that if I worked hard in school, my parents would be proud of me because they wanted to see me do my best in my studies.12 And because of my hard work in school, from Grade 9 to Grade 12, I received the “With High Honors” award.13 This was an award I once thought I could never achieve, but through my perseverance, I received it consistently.14 I am also proud to have graduated from Senior High School as the Top 1 of our strand.15 These were also the times when my understanding of love grew deeper.16 I made mistakes and experienced several rejections from the girls I loved.17 But all of those became learning experiences for me, and I learned to become more mature when it comes to relationships.18 My teenage years taught me to become a better person and to keep improving myself.19 I got to know myself better as I faced challenges and worked hard in my studies and in life.20


College Years

My current experience in college as a 2nd year Bachelor of Science in Information Technology student studying at the University of the East - Manila under the Computer Studies and Systems Department, is one of my biggest stages in life.1 My responsibilities and time for studying increased because of the many activities, assignments, quizzes, and exams.2 But I was able to handle all of these because I believed in my ability to pass all my subjects and get high grades.3 In my first year, I didn’t struggle much because my subjects were not that difficult for me yet.4 Some of my classmates were the same people I had in Senior High School, and I also met new classmates who became an important part of my current college journey.5 In college, I also realized that I need to be more careful in choosing my circle of friends and who I should work with in group activities.6 I prefer to be with people like me who take their studies seriously because it increases the chances of getting good grades.7 Being a college student is extremely stressful because of the many deadlines I have to meet.8 There are times when I don’t get to sleep because I still need to finish activities with very short deadlines.9 Often, I spend the whole night reviewing lessons that come with countless PDF slides.10 All of this is exhausting, but I remain calm and don’t let my emotions take over because I know I just need to do everything step by step, despite working under pressure.11 I also realized that I shouldn’t stay in my comfort zone and that I need to broaden my perspective in the things I do.12 I learned that I should focus on growing myself because, at the end of the day, I am the one who will help myself.13 I realized that I shouldn’t just follow what my friends do, so I created my own ways to survive college life.14 Being a college student made me realize that I am truly growing up, as the responsibilities I face are increasing, but all of these are part of my dreams.15 I can also see myself more clearly on the path I am taking, which is becoming an IT specialist.16 There are still many subjects and challenges I need to go through to achieve my dreams in life.17 But I believe I can overcome all of that because I trust in my abilities and in God’s plan.18 In just a few years, I will finish this course, and even though we still have a thesis to complete, I believe I can pass it and fulfill the dreams I have for myself and my family.19 I will do my best to achieve the life I want.20 Padayon!
